ЁЯкР Detailed Information About Mars

ЁЯФ╣ 1. Basic Facts

  • English Name: Mars
  • Bengali Name: ржоржЩрзНржЧрж▓ ржЧрзНрж░рж╣ (Mongol Groho)
  • Position from Sun: Fourth planet
  • Average Distance from Sun: Approx. 228 million kilometers
  • Diameter: Approx. 6,792 kilometers (about half of Earth's)
  • Mass: 10.7% of Earth's Mass
  • Volume: Approx. 15% of Earth's Volume
  • Gravity: 38% of Earth's gravity (Weight will feel much less)

ЁЯФ╣ 2. Surface Features

Mars' surface is reddish due to the high amount of **iron oxide (rust)** present. It is dry, dusty, and rocky. It features mountains, volcanoes, valleys, polar ice caps, and craters. **Olympus Mons**, the largest volcano in the Solar System, is on MarsтАФits height is about 25 km (three times Mount Everest). The largest canyon is **Valles Marineris**, about 4,000 km long.

ЁЯФ╣ 3. Atmosphere

The atmosphere is extremely thin, about 100 times less dense than Earth's. **Primary components:** Carbon dioxide (COтВВ) тАУ 95%, Nitrogen (NтВВ) тАУ 2.7%, Argon (Ar) тАУ 1.6%, Oxygen (OтВВ) тАУ 0.13%, Water vapor (HтВВO) тАУ trace amounts. This thin atmosphere cannot retain heat, resulting in massive temperature differences between day and night.

ЁЯФ╣ 4. Temperature

Average Temperature: -60┬░C. Daytime: Can rise up to approx. 20┬░C. Nighttime: Drops to -100┬░C to -125┬░C. Therefore, insulation for heat-tolerant equipment and robots is very important on Mars.

ЁЯФ╣ 5. Martian Day and Year (Time)

One Day (Sol): 24 hours, 39 minutes, 35 seconds. One Year: 687 Earth days. That is, a day on Mars is slightly longer than on Earth, and a year is nearly twice as long.

ЁЯФ╣ 6. Moons

Mars has two small moons: 1. **Phobos** and 2. **Deimos**. Both are rocky and irregularly shaped, believed to be captured asteroids.

ЁЯФ╣ 7. Water and Ice

Water ice layers (Water Ice + Dry Ice) exist at Mars' polar regions. There is a possibility of liquid water beneath the surface. It is believed that rivers and lakes flowed on Mars in the past.

ЁЯФ╣ 8. Energy and Light

Solar light on Mars is 43% less intense than on Earth. However, electricity generation is possible with solar panelsтАФNASA's robots operate this way.

ЁЯФ╣ 9. Communication and Distance

Messages take 4тАУ24 minutes to travel from Earth to Mars (depending on orbital distance). Therefore, robots require AI systems capable of autonomous operation.

ЁЯФ╣ 10. Human and Robotic Missions

First successful mission: Mariner 4 (NASA, 1965). Key missions include: Viking 1 & 2 (1976), Mars Pathfinder (Sojourner Rover, 1997), Spirit & Opportunity (2004), Curiosity Rover (2012), Perseverance Rover (2021), Ingenuity Helicopter.

ЁЯФ╣ 11. Future Possibilities

Scientists believe humans will be able to live on Mars in the future. Technologies are being developed for artificial habitats, food production systems, water harvesting, and energy generation. SpaceX and NASA are working towards this goal.
